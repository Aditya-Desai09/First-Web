MAPS

Review: Valorant maps serve as the virtual battlegrounds where the game's rounds unfold. Each map has a unique layout and design, featuring bombsites, spawn points, and various pathways. The primary objectives involve planting or preventing the plant of the Spike at designated bombsites. Tactical opportunities abound, with diverse elements such as chokepoints, open areas, and elevated positions. Callouts are essential for effective communication, and some maps have unique features like ropes or ziplines. Players must understand each map's layout and strategic nuances to succeed in matches. Popular maps include Bind, Split, Haven, Ascent, and Icebox, each contributing to a dynamic and engaging gameplay experience.

Familiarity with callouts and map-specific features enhances players' effectiveness in matches. The map pool may change periodically as new maps are introduced or rotated in and out.

ASCENT :

Ascent was one of the first maps added to Valorant and remains a player favorite.According to Valorant lore, this map was constructed on top of San Marco in Venice, drawing significant inspiration from Italian architecture and streets, as evident in the visuals. Renowned for its captivating design; Ascent stands out as one of the most aesthetically pleasing maps in Valorant.
